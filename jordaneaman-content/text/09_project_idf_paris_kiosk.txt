IDF PARIS KIOSK - MOMO
=======================

PROJECT TITLE:
MOMO - Urban news kiosk redesign for Paris Île-de-France

CLIENT:
Paris Île-de-France (IDF) regional council, in partnership with Valérie Pécresse's office

DESIGN CHALLENGE:
The project tackled reimagining classical Parisian newsstands with the central question: "How can we foster a unique, local, social spot that connects citizen and information flows?"

METHODOLOGY:
The team conducted informal interviews with consumers and kiosk attendants to understand stakeholder needs before beginning the design process.

SOLUTION: MOMO
The designers created an innovative kiosk concept featuring:
- Spatial sketches showing scene views, floor plans, and aerial perspectives
- An interaction mapping diagram illustrating user engagement patterns
- Design focused on fostering connection between citizens and local information

DELIVERABLES:
The page showcases spatial sketches (including kioskScene, kioskFloorplan, kioskCaisse, and kioskAerialView) and a comprehensive interaction map visualizing how people move through and use the space.

PROJECT DESCRIPTION:
This project demonstrates user-centered design methodology applied to public infrastructure, balancing functional information distribution with social gathering spaces.
